About Me

Hello! I am a recent graduate of Columbia University, where I received an MS in Computer Science (2019), and an BA in Computer Science with minors in Linguistics and Economics (2018). My research interests are in natural language processing (NLP), speech, and artificial intelligence. Specifically, I hope to develop systems that are robust, generalizable, and informed by both machine learning methods and linguistic theory.

I am currently an Applied Scientist Intern at Amazon, working on abstractive summarization models. I am also involved in several ongoing research projects.

At Columbia, I have been advised by Prof. Julia Hirschberg at the Spoken Language Processing Group, and have worked on developing natural-sounding text-to-speech systems. I have also been fortunate enough to work with Prof. Kathleen McKeown at Columbia, Prof. Jesse Harris at UCLA, and Dr. Andreas Stolcke at Microsoft.

In addition to research, am also dedicated towards teaching, serving as teaching assistant for many computer science courses, from introductory to graduate-level.

I will be applying for PhD programs in Computer Science this fall.
